Unary Operators

Unary Operators takes a single operand/arguement and performs an operation. We have the unary plus (+) which tries to convert the operand into a number. The unary Negation (-) tries to convert the operand into a number and negates after. Logical not (!) converts to a boolean value then negates it. Increment (++) and decrement (--) adds and subtracts one from the operand. Then there is the typeof which gives the data type of the operand and delete which removes the variable
